Outcomes-Centric Solutions to Transform Pharmacy Care

Ready to Transform Your Pharmacy?

 

Seamless Med Management

As health systems continue to expand, the need for optimized pharmacy care to support a growing enterprise is more important than ever. Pharmacy leaders recognize the opportunity of centralizing pharmacy operations but lack the best practice and industry resources to help build a comprehensive strategy to support both inpatient and outpatient needs.

Our latest Illuminate event brings together an exclusive group of industry leaders who are realizing the clinical, financial, and operational benefits of seamless medication management across all settings of care through a central fill pharmacy approach and entity-owned specialty pharmacy. You won’t want to miss this dynamic conversation!

Speakers

Central Fill Pharmacy

Nilesh Desai

Chief Pharmacy Officer

Baptist Health

Nilesh Desai is the Chief Pharmacy Officer for Baptist Health System. In this role, he is responsible for the pharmacy enterprise operations across the nine-hospital system, including oversight of the system’s ambulatory pharmacies, specialty pharmacy, research pharmacy, pharmacy technology and automation, value based care pharmacy, and medication management for all the clinics. In this role, Nilesh has expanded the Clinical Pharmacists team to include specialties such as Emergency Medicine, Organ Transplant, Oncology, Psychiatry, and more. He also has expanded the PGY-1 Pharmacy residency rogram with the addition of a PGY-2 Emergency Medicince Program. Nilesh has also implemented a Transitions of Care program with pharmacist-led medication reconciliation for inpatients and meds to beds for ambulatory patients across the system.

Nilesh has led implementation of multiple technology projects, centralizing and integrating all decision making on technology, training, educational materials, formulary standardization, medication management policies, workflows, EPIC build and standardization across the system for the pharmacy enterprise. He was directly responsible for the planning and opening of a 102,000 square foot Central Pharmacy Services Center (CPSC) in 2024, designed to expand services through improved infrastructure and efficiencies, ultimately increasing access to ongoing quality care.

Prior to joining Baptist Health System Nilesh spent 21 years with Hackensack University Medical Center/Hackensack Meridian Health in New Jersey, as administrator of pharmacy and clinical operations in addition to numerous other pharmacy leadership roles. He is a recipient of The Gregory A. Santora New Jersey Pharmacist Leadership of the Year Award and HealthTrust Clinical Excellence Award for Transitions of Care. He has presented at many conferences nationally, participated in many studies, and is a member of many national, regional and local pharmacy practice committees. Nilesh holds a Bachelor of Science Degree in Pharmacy and a Masters in Business Administration (Healthcare). He is also a Certified Professional in Patient Safety.

Specialty Pharmacy

Abhinav Rastogi

President and CEO/EVP

Temple University Hospital/Temple Health

Abhinav “Abhi” Rastogi is the President and CEO of Temple University Hospital and EVP of Temple Health. He has a proven leadership around operational efficiency initiatives with sustained outcomes and results. He is a forward thinking, solutions focused and outcomes driven leader who combines project management, Lean Six Sigma methodologies, data analytics and leadership to execute strategic clinical and operational improvement initiatives. He has a strong background in leading and growing clinical service lines and was instrumental in the growth of the Temple Lung Transplant program to the top volume program in the country. In addition, he oversees Solid Organ Transplants, Cardiology, Neurology, Gastroenterology, Lab, Pharmacy and Supply Chain service lines. His strong focus on Supply Chain operations has contributed to over $10M savings annually and has presented supply chain efficiency opportunities at several national conferences.

Due to his strong technical background, Abhi works with Healthcare Tech startups to be able to provide better solutions to patients / providers and offer Temple University Hospital as an incubator for development and research. The recent Broncoscopic Lung Volume Reduction (BLVR) valve approved for commercial use was evaluated in most patients at Temple. In addition, he focuses on remote monitoring of patients with connected devices to evaluate care and improve outcomes.

Abhi has an MBA with a concentration in eBusiness and Healthcare Finance, MS degree in Information Systems and Bachelor of Engineering degree in Electronics/Computers. He is also a certified Project Management Professional and a Master Black Belt in Lean Six Sigma. During his 19 years at Temple, Abhi has served as director of the Project Management Office, Associate Hospital Director of the Pulmonary Service Line, and Senior Vice President Professional Services. Before that, he was a pharmaco-economic data analyst at Temple University’s School of Pharmacy, a consultant for IBM, and a project team leader at Tata Infotech.

Jillian Foster

System Pharmacy and Radiology Service Line Administrator

Baptist Memorial Health Care Corporation

As System Pharmacy and Radiology Service Line Administrator for Baptist Memorial Health Care Corporation (BMHCC) Jillian Foster provides strategic direction to the BMHCC pharmacy teams at all Baptist hospitals, infusion centers, retail pharmacies, home infusion, mail order and specialty pharmacy in Missouri, Tennessee, and Arkansas. She also works with Radiology leaders and Imaging locations across the system on growth and patient care opportunities. Jillian graduated from the University of Mississippi School of Pharmacy after completing a Health Policy Fellowship with United States Senator Thad Cochran. She also completed a Pharmacy Practice Residency (PGY1) and Specialty Residency (PGY2) with an emphasis in Pharmacy Management and Administration at North Mississippi Medical Center (NMMC) in Tupelo, MS. She worked in Tupelo as the Pharmacy Benefits Manager at NMMC before moving to Oxford, MS as the Director of Pharmacy for Baptist Memorial Hospital- North Mississippi. She later held leadership positions at Baptist Medical Group and the Baptist Cancer Center before moving to the Baptist Corporate office. Jillian served on the Phi Lambda Sigma National Executive Committee as Parliamentarian. She is Past President of the Mississippi Pharmacists Association and the Mississippi Society of Health System Pharmacy. Jillian is currently serving on the Mississippi Board of Pharmacy. She and her husband, Doug, live in Olive Branch, MS and enjoy many church, community, and sports activities with their three children.

Building for the Future: Launching a Central Pharmacy Services Center for Health System Enterprise

Nilesh Desai, Chief Pharmacy Officer
Baptist Health
As healthcare systems evolve to meet increasing demands for efficiency and patient care quality, the role of Central Pharmacy Services Centers (CSCs) has become more crucial than ever. Nilesh Desai, Chief Pharmacy Officer at Baptist Health, will share insights from his recent implementation of a CSC, detailing the transformative impact on their operations.

This session will explore the strategic planning, challenges, and solutions involved in establishing a CSC that aligns with modern healthcare demands. Nilesh will discuss the critical elements of designing a CSC, from selecting the right technology and automation, to partnering with vendors that support long-term operational goals.

Join us to learn how CSCs are reshaping healthcare, offering scalable solutions that not only meet current needs but also anticipate future challenges, ultimately enhancing patient care.

Optimizing Specialty Pharmacy for Best-in-Class Patient Care and Financial Growth

Abhinav Rastogi, President and CEO/EVP
Temple University Hospital/Temple Health
Jillian Foster, System Pharmacy and Radiology Service Line Administrator
Baptist Memorial Health Care Corporation
Lani Bertrand, Senior Director, Clinical Marketing and Thought Leadership
Omnicell
While hospital operating margins have started to rebound post-pandemic, challenges such as staffing shortages and rising expenses persist. Specialty Pharmacy is an increasingly important opportunity to weather these financial headwinds. However, even hospitals with existing specialty pharmacies often fall short of the potential for financial performance, operational efficiency, and patient care. In this panel discussion, leaders from Temple Health and Baptist Memorial Health Care Corporation will share proven strategies for both implementing and optimizing an entity-owned specialty pharmacy in this era of strained healthcare resources.
